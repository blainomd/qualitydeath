Quality of Death Is a Measure. Not a Metaphor.

The Economist Intelligence Unit ranks countries on how well they die. The US ranks 43rd. We can do better — and it starts with a conversation.

What Quality of Death Means

Quality of death measures whether people die in the setting they choose, with the care they want, after conversations that matter. It is a clinical outcome, tracked by researchers and health systems worldwide.

Do you die where you want to? 80% want to die at home. 60% die in hospitals.
Are your wishes documented? Only 37% of adults have an advance directive.
Does your family know your preferences? Most don't — and the cost is measured in guilt, conflict, and unwanted interventions.
Is pain managed? Palliative care reduces suffering and extends life — but only 14% of eligible patients receive it.

The Conversation That Changes Everything

Advance care planning is not about death. It's about clarity.

What matters most to you in the last chapter of your life?

Who should make decisions when you can't?

What treatments would you want — and which would you refuse?

Where do you want to be cared for?

These conversations take 30 minutes. They save families from years of regret.
