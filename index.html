Where to start

Three things families can do.

You don't have to do all of this at once. Start with whatever feels most urgent.

One

Document your preferences now

An advance directive tells your medical team what you want — and what you don't want — when you can't speak for yourself. It takes about 20 minutes to complete. It works in all 50 states. The hardest part is starting.

Two

Find the right care team

The right care team knows your wishes before a crisis forces the question. co-op.care connects families with worker-owned caregivers trained to support people at home — on their terms, in the time they have left.

Three

Give caregivers permission to honor your wishes

The people who love you shouldn't have to guess. A care plan shared with your family, your proxy, and your care team means that when the moment comes, the people in the room know exactly what to do.

Your values

What matters to you?

An advance directive isn't a legal document. It's a values document. Before you fill out a form, it helps to sit with a few honest questions.

"If I could only have one thing at the end of my life, it would be …"
"The thing I'm most afraid of is not the dying. It's …"
"Quality of life, to me, means I can still …"
"I want the people I love to know that I …"
Explore your values at CareGoals

CareGoals guides you through these questions and turns your answers into a shareable care plan.
